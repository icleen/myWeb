Projects:

My current research is focused on the 1940 U.S. census. The goal of my team is to be able to read in a page from the census and automatically draw information from it. This allows the documents to be searchable, which is helpful for people trying to do family history research. It also allows us to draw a lot of data that economists and historians can use to learn generalities about American citizens during that time period. Another project I worked on was training a neural network to recognize Japanese Characters (kanji). In the end I was able to get about 96% accuracy on the data set I was using, which is pretty good. I also worked on a document view tool. In most Asian countries they have pedigree charts for each family name that can stretch back hundreds of years. They are structured so you can easily see the family hierarchy. The tool I worked on made use of that structure and displayed those documents in a format so that you could see the hierarchy clearly.
